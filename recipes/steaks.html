Cast Iron Ribeye Steaks

The best way to cook steak, period!

This is simply the best way to cook steak. It adds a ton of flavor and can make even cheap ribeyes taste like hundred dollar masterpieces from your favorite steakhouse.

Ingredients

  • Ribeye Steak(s)
  • Coarse ground pepper (or fresh-cracked)
  • Kosher salt (larger grain works best)
  • Fresh rosemary
  • Fresh thyme
  • 3-4 cloves of garlic per steak
  • Kerrygold butter (don't skimp here)
  • Avocado oil (or another high smoke point oil

Instructions

  1. Strip and smash 3-4 garlic cloves per steak
  2. Season steaks to taste with salt and pepper (don't be afraid to go heavy)
  3. Put avocado oil in cast-iron skillet and heat skillet to 500+ degrees surface temp
  4. Place steak on skillet, pressing down firmly to get sizzling surface contact
  5. Flip steak after 1-1.5 minutes (depending on thickness and weight). You're looking for a dark crust on the opposite side when flipped
  6. Add butter, garlic, rosemary, and thyme to skillet
  7. Every 10-15 seconds, baste steak with melted butter that has the aromatics soaking in it
  8. Pull steak when it reaches desired internal temp - another minute and a half will usually yield medium rare (135) - account for carryover cooking
  9. Pour skillet contents onto steak and allow to rest for 10-15 minutes
