Texas-Style Brisket

Brisket done right!

Texas is known for its rub-style bbq, and nothing represents that more than Texas-style brisket - a thick bark of salt and pepper with a masterful smoke flavor

Ingredients

  • Brisket flat or full packer brisket
  • Coarse ground pepper (or fresh-cracked)
  • Kosher salt (larger grain works best)
  • Binder (mustard, worcestershire sauce, etc)

Instructions

  1. Trim brisket, removing any silverskin and large fat deposits. Trim fatcap to 1/4 inch
  2. Apply binder liberally to brisket
  3. Thickly coat brisket with 50/50 salt and pepper mix
  4. Allow brisket to rest and rise to room temp while in this rub mixture
  5. Depending on personal time, set smoker to cook as low and slow as you safely can (I prefer 225, others 275 to cook faster
  6. Put brisket on smoker
  7. Monitor temp, when the "stall" is reached (155-175, noticeable slowdown in temperature gains) then remove brisket and wrap in pink butcher paper.
  8. You can add things to the wrap, but I do not. The brisket goes back on the smoker fully wrapped
  9. Monitor until brisket reaches 203+ degrees and is probe tender (sticking your temperature probe in should feel like putting a toothpick into warm butter)
